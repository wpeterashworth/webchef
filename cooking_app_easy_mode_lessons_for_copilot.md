# Category 1: Ingredient Substitutions

## Skill: Dairy Swaps

### Mini Lesson 1: Dairy Does More Than Add Liquid

Dairy ingredients can do different jobs depending on the recipe. Some dairy ingredients mainly add moisture, while others add richness, thickness, or a slightly tangy flavor.

Before choosing a substitute, it helps to ask what the dairy is doing in the recipe. For example, a thin dairy ingredient is easier to replace when the recipe only needs liquid. A thicker or tangier dairy ingredient needs a substitute that is closer in texture or flavor.

#### Question 1

A pancake recipe calls for 1 cup of buttermilk. Which substitution works best?

A. 1 cup milk mixed with a small amount of lemon juice or vinegar  
B. 1 cup water  
C. 1 cup melted butter  
D. 1 cup flour mixed with sugar  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Buttermilk adds moisture and acidity. Milk with a little lemon juice or vinegar is a closer match than water or butter.

### Mini Lesson 2: Match Texture and Flavor

A good dairy substitute should be similar to the original ingredient. Texture matters because some dairy ingredients are thin and pourable, while others are thick and spoonable. Flavor matters too, especially when the dairy is used as a topping or dip.

For toppings, dips, and spreads, the substitute usually needs to stay thick enough to sit on the food instead of soaking into it. A substitute with a mild tangy flavor can also work better than something plain or watery.

#### Question 2

Which ingredient is the best easy substitute for sour cream in a baked potato topping?

A. Plain Greek yogurt  
B. Whole milk  
C. Melted butter  
D. Water  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Plain Greek yogurt is thick and slightly tangy, so it works better as a sour cream substitute than thin liquids like milk or water.

### Mini Lesson 3: Some Swaps Change the Final Dish

Even when a substitution works, it can still change the final result. A recipe may turn out thinner, less creamy, less rich, or slightly different in flavor.

This matters most in soups, sauces, dips, and desserts, where dairy often affects texture. When replacing a rich dairy ingredient, choose something that can replace both the liquid and some of the fat. Otherwise, the dish may still cook, but it may not feel as creamy.

#### Question 3

Which substitute works best for 1 cup of heavy cream in a soup when the cream does not need to be whipped?

A. 1 cup milk with butter  
B. 1 cup water  
C. 1 cup plain broth only  
D. 1 cup lemon juice  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Heavy cream adds both liquid and fat. Milk with butter is a better match because it helps keep the soup creamy and rich.

## Skill: Herb & Spice Alts

### Mini Lesson 1: Fresh and Dried Herbs Are Different

Fresh herbs and dried herbs can both add flavor, but they are not used in the same amount. Dried herbs usually have a stronger, more concentrated flavor because the water has been removed.

When swapping dried herbs for fresh herbs, you usually need less dried herb than fresh herb. A common rule is to use about one-third as much dried herb as fresh. This helps keep the flavor from becoming too strong.

#### Question 1

A recipe calls for 1 tablespoon of fresh basil, but you only have dried basil. What should you use?

A. 1 teaspoon dried basil  
B. 1 tablespoon dried basil  
C. 3 tablespoons dried basil  
D. 1 cup dried basil  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Dried herbs are stronger than fresh herbs, so you usually use less. Since 1 tablespoon equals 3 teaspoons, 1 teaspoon dried basil is about one-third of the fresh amount.

### Mini Lesson 2: Think About the Herb’s Job

Herbs can be used for different reasons. Sometimes they add strong flavor while the food cooks. Other times, they are added at the end to make the dish look fresh, colorful, or finished.

When an herb is mostly being used as a garnish, the substitute should usually be fresh, mild, and green. A dried herb may add flavor, but it will not give the same fresh look or texture.

#### Question 2

Which herb is the best substitute for fresh parsley when it is mainly used as a garnish?

A. Fresh cilantro  
B. Ground cinnamon  
C. Garlic powder  
D. Dried bay leaf  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Fresh cilantro is green and leafy, so it can work as a garnish. The other options do not provide the same fresh appearance or texture.

### Mini Lesson 3: Some Seasonings Are Blends

Some seasonings are not single ingredients. They are blends made from several herbs or spices mixed together. If you are missing a seasoning blend, you may be able to replace it by combining spices that create a similar flavor.

When replacing a blend, think about the type of flavor it usually adds. Some blends are warm and sweet, some are savory and herbal, and some are smoky or spicy.

#### Question 3

Which mixture can replace 1 teaspoon of pumpkin pie spice?

A. Cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and cloves  
B. Salt, pepper, garlic powder, and onion powder  
C. Basil, oregano, thyme, and rosemary  
D. Chili powder, cumin, paprika, and cayenne  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Pumpkin pie spice is a warm baking spice blend. Cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and cloves create a similar warm flavor.

## Skill: Baking Binders & Thickeners

### Mini Lesson 1: Binders Hold Ingredients Together

Some ingredients act like glue in a recipe. These are called binders. A binder helps ingredients stick together so the food does not crumble or fall apart.

Eggs are common binders in baked goods like muffins, cookies, and quick breads. If a recipe needs an egg mainly for binding, the substitute should also help hold the mixture together.

#### Question 1

Which mixture can replace one egg as a binder in muffins or cookies?

A. 1 tablespoon ground flaxseed mixed with 3 tablespoons water  
B. 1 cup water  
C. 1 tablespoon salt mixed with 3 tablespoons sugar  
D. 1 cup flour  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Ground flaxseed mixed with water creates a gel-like mixture that can help bind ingredients together in baked goods.

### Mini Lesson 2: Thickeners Make Mixtures Less Runny

Thickeners help liquids become thicker. They are often used in sauces, gravies, soups, puddings, and fruit fillings.

Cornstarch and flour can both be used as thickeners, but they are not always used in the same amount. Cornstarch has stronger thickening power than flour, so you usually need more flour if you are using it as a substitute.

#### Question 2

A sauce recipe calls for 1 tablespoon of cornstarch, but you only have all-purpose flour. What should you use?

A. 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour  
B. 1 teaspoon all-purpose flour  
C. 1 cup all-purpose flour  
D. 2 tablespoons sugar  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Flour is a weaker thickener than cornstarch, so you usually need about twice as much flour to replace cornstarch.

### Mini Lesson 3: Technique Matters With Thickeners

Using the right thickener is important, but how you add it matters too. Some starches can clump if they are added directly to hot liquid.

A slurry is a mixture of starch and cold liquid. Mixing the starch with cold water first helps it spread evenly before it goes into a hot sauce, soup, or filling.

#### Question 3

A recipe calls for a cornstarch slurry. What is the correct way to prepare the substitute before adding it to hot liquid?

A. Mix the starch with cold water first  
B. Sprinkle dry starch directly into boiling liquid  
C. Mix the starch with oil only  
D. Bake the starch before adding it  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A slurry is made by mixing starch with cold liquid first. This helps prevent clumps when it is added to hot liquid.

# Category 2: Recipe Adjustment

## Skill: Scaling Ratios

### Mini Lesson 1: Doubling a Recipe

Scaling a recipe means changing the amount of food the recipe makes. If you want to make twice as much food, you usually double the ingredients.

Doubling means multiplying the original amount by 2. This works well for basic ingredients like broth, pasta, flour, rice, or vegetables.

#### Question 1

A soup recipe serves 4 people and uses 2 cups of broth. How much broth is needed to serve 8 people?

A. 1 cup  
B. 2 cups  
C. 4 cups  
D. 8 cups  

Correct Answer: C

Feedback: The recipe is being doubled from 4 servings to 8 servings, so the broth also doubles from 2 cups to 4 cups.

### Mini Lesson 2: Cutting a Recipe in Half

Sometimes you may want to make less food instead of more. If you want to make half as much food, you usually cut the ingredients in half.

Halving means dividing the original amount by 2. This is useful when a recipe makes too many servings or when you only have enough ingredients for a smaller batch.

#### Question 2

A cookie recipe makes 24 cookies and requires 3 cups of flour. How much flour is needed to make 12 cookies?

A. 1 1/2 cups  
B. 2 cups  
C. 3 cups  
D. 6 cups  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: The recipe is being cut in half from 24 cookies to 12 cookies, so the flour is also cut in half from 3 cups to 1 1/2 cups.

### Mini Lesson 3: Keep the Same Ratio

Some recipes depend on ingredients staying in the same balance. This is called keeping the same ratio. If one ingredient increases, the other connected ingredients need to increase in the same way.

This matters in recipes like bread, sauces, dressings, and batters. If the ratio changes too much, the food may become too dry, too wet, too thick, or too thin.

#### Question 3

A bread recipe uses 4 cups of flour and 2 cups of water. If you use 6 cups of flour, how much water keeps the same ratio?

A. 2 cups  
B. 3 cups  
C. 4 cups  
D. 6 cups  

Correct Answer: B

Feedback: The original recipe uses half as much water as flour. If the flour increases to 6 cups, the water should increase to 3 cups to keep the same ratio.

## Skill: Emergency Flavor Tuning

### Mini Lesson 1: Start With the Most Likely Fix

When food tastes bland, it usually does not need a complicated fix right away. Often, it needs a small amount of seasoning. Salt is one of the most important flavor tools because it helps bring out the flavors that are already in the food.

The best first step is to add a little, stir, and taste again. Adding too much at once can make the food too salty, so small adjustments are safer.

#### Question 1

A pot of vegetable soup tastes bland even though the ingredients are fully cooked. What should you try first?

A. Add a small amount of salt, stir, and taste again  
B. Add a full cup of sugar  
C. Throw the soup away and start over  
D. Add uncooked pasta directly to the soup  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Bland food often needs a little more seasoning. Adding a small amount of salt and tasting again is safer than making a large change all at once.

### Mini Lesson 2: Calm Down Spicy Heat

If a dish tastes too spicy, the goal is to reduce how strong the heat feels. Spicy flavors can often be softened by adding something creamy, fatty, or mild.

Ingredients like yogurt, sour cream, coconut milk, cream, or extra mild ingredients can help balance heat. This does not remove the spice completely, but it can make the dish easier to eat.

#### Question 2

A curry tastes too spicy for your guests. What is the best way to reduce the perceived heat?

A. Add a creamy ingredient like yogurt or coconut milk  
B. Add more chili powder  
C. Add a large amount of black pepper  
D. Boil it until the spice disappears  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Creamy or fatty ingredients can help soften the feeling of spicy heat. Adding more spicy ingredients would make the problem worse.

### Mini Lesson 3: Fix Saltiness by Spreading It Out

If a dish is too salty, adding sugar usually does not solve the problem. The most reliable fix is to spread the salt out by adding more unsalted ingredients or more liquid.

For soups and stews, this might mean adding unsalted broth, water, vegetables, beans, rice, or potatoes. The goal is to make a larger amount of food so the salty flavor is less concentrated.

#### Question 3

A stew tastes too salty. Which emergency adjustment is most effective?

A. Add more unsalted ingredients or liquid  
B. Add more salt  
C. Add extra hot sauce  
D. Add baking powder  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Saltiness is best reduced by diluting or stretching the dish with unsalted ingredients or liquid. This spreads the salt through more food.

## Skill: Texture Fixes

### Mini Lesson 1: Thickening Food That Is Too Thin

Sometimes soups, sauces, or gravies turn out thinner than expected. The goal is to thicken the food without making it lumpy.

One safe way to thicken a hot liquid is to make a slurry. A slurry is made by mixing a thickener, like cornstarch or flour, with a small amount of cold liquid first. Once it is smooth, it can be stirred into the hot food.

#### Question 1

A soup is much thinner than intended. What is the best way to thicken it quickly without creating lumps?

A. Mix a thickener with cold liquid first, then stir it into the soup  
B. Dump dry flour directly into the hot soup  
C. Add ice cubes to the soup  
D. Turn off the heat and add sugar  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Mixing the thickener with cold liquid first helps prevent lumps. This makes it easier to stir smoothly into the soup.

### Mini Lesson 2: Thinning Food That Is Too Thick

Sometimes a sauce, soup, or batter becomes thicker than you want. The best fix is to add a small amount of liquid at a time.

Adding liquid slowly gives you more control. If you add too much at once, the food can quickly become too thin. Stir after each small addition and check the texture before adding more.

#### Question 2

A cooked sauce is too thick. What is the best way to thin it while maintaining control of the texture?

A. Add a small amount of liquid at a time and stir  
B. Add a full cup of liquid immediately  
C. Add dry flour  
D. Cook it longer without stirring  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Adding liquid slowly helps you control the texture. A large amount of liquid could make the sauce too thin.

### Mini Lesson 3: Fixing Watery Foods

Some foods become watery when they have too much liquid or not enough structure. This can happen with foods like mashed potatoes, rice, sauces, or casseroles.

To fix watery food, you usually need to either absorb the extra moisture or let some of it cook off. The best choice depends on the food. For mashed potatoes, adding more potato flakes or cooked potato can help absorb extra moisture and make the texture thicker.

#### Question 3

Mashed potatoes have become too watery. Which adjustment is most likely to improve their texture?

A. Add more cooked potato or potato flakes  
B. Add more water  
C. Add ice  
D. Add vinegar  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Cooked potato or potato flakes can absorb extra moisture and help thicken watery mashed potatoes.

# Category 3: How to Cook

## Skill: Sautéing & Pan-Searing

### Mini Lesson 1: Sautéing vs. Pan-Searing

Sautéing and pan-searing both use a pan, heat, and a small amount of oil, but they are used for different results.

Sautéing usually means cooking smaller pieces of food while stirring or moving them around in the pan. It is often used for vegetables, garlic, onions, or small pieces of meat.

Pan-searing usually means cooking food over higher heat to create a browned crust on the outside. It is often used for larger pieces of meat, fish, tofu, or vegetables.

#### Question 1

What is the main difference between sautéing and pan-searing?

A. Sautéing usually cooks smaller pieces while moving them, while pan-searing creates a browned crust  
B. Sautéing only uses water, while pan-searing only uses sugar  
C. Sautéing is always done in the oven, while pan-searing is always done in the microwave  
D. Sautéing means freezing food before cooking it  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Sautéing usually involves smaller pieces that are moved around in the pan. Pan-searing focuses on browning the outside of the food.

### Mini Lesson 2: Do Not Overcrowd the Pan

Food needs space in the pan to cook well. When too much food is added at once, the pan temperature can drop and moisture gets trapped.

Instead of browning, overcrowded food may steam. This can make vegetables softer and meat less browned. Cooking in smaller batches can help food brown better and cook more evenly.

#### Question 2

What usually happens when too much food is placed in a sauté pan at once?

A. The food may steam instead of brown  
B. The food instantly becomes crispy  
C. The pan gets colder forever  
D. The food stops cooking completely  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Too much food can trap moisture and lower the pan temperature. This often causes food to steam instead of brown.

### Mini Lesson 3: Dry Food Browns Better

Moisture makes browning harder. If meat is wet on the surface, the pan has to evaporate that moisture before browning can really begin.

Patting meat dry with a paper towel helps the surface brown more easily. This is especially useful when pan-searing chicken, steak, pork, fish, or tofu.

#### Question 3

Why should meat usually be patted dry before pan-searing?

A. To help the surface brown better  
B. To make the meat colder  
C. To remove all flavor from the meat  
D. To make the pan stop heating  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A dry surface browns better because the pan can focus on searing instead of evaporating extra moisture.

## Skill: Boiling, Simmering, & Poaching

### Mini Lesson 1: Recognizing a Full Boil

Boiling is one of the strongest forms of cooking with liquid. A full boil has large bubbles that rise quickly and constantly break the surface.

Boiling is useful for foods that can handle stronger movement, like pasta, potatoes, or some vegetables. Since the water is moving a lot, boiling is not always the best choice for delicate foods.

#### Question 1

Which description best matches a full boil?

A. Large bubbles constantly rising and breaking the surface  
B. No bubbles at all  
C. A few tiny bubbles sitting at the bottom of the pot  
D. Food cooking without any liquid  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A full boil has strong, active bubbles that keep rising and breaking the surface of the liquid.

### Mini Lesson 2: Simmering Is Gentler Than Boiling

Simmering uses lower heat than boiling. Instead of large, rapid bubbles, a simmer has smaller bubbles that rise more gently.

Simmering is useful when food needs time to cook without being tossed around too much. Soups, stews, sauces, and beans are often simmered because gentle cooking helps flavors develop while keeping the texture under control.

#### Question 2

How does simmering differ from boiling?

A. Simmering uses gentler heat with smaller bubbles  
B. Simmering means cooking with no heat  
C. Simmering only happens in the freezer  
D. Simmering uses larger bubbles than boiling  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Simmering is gentler than boiling. It uses lower heat and smaller bubbles.

### Mini Lesson 3: Poaching Uses Very Gentle Heat

Poaching is even gentler than simmering. The liquid is hot, but it should not bubble strongly. This makes poaching useful for delicate foods that could break apart, dry out, or become tough with stronger heat.

Foods like eggs, fish, or tender chicken can be poached because the gentle liquid cooks them slowly and evenly.

#### Question 3

Which cooking method uses liquid kept below a simmer to cook delicate foods gently?

A. Poaching  
B. Frying  
C. Roasting  
D. Freezing  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Poaching uses hot liquid below a simmer. It is gentle enough for delicate foods like eggs or fish.

## Skill: Oven Roasting vs. Baking

### Mini Lesson 1: Roasting and Baking Use the Oven Differently

Roasting and baking both use dry heat in the oven, but they are usually used for different types of food.

Roasting is often used for foods that already have structure, like vegetables, potatoes, chicken, or meat. The goal is often to brown the outside and develop deeper flavor.

Baking is often used for foods that need to set or rise, like cookies, cakes, muffins, and bread. These foods usually depend more on careful measuring and timing.

#### Question 1

Which statement best explains the traditional difference between roasting and baking?

A. Roasting is often used for structured foods like vegetables or meats, while baking is often used for foods like breads, cakes, or cookies  
B. Roasting only uses cold air, while baking only uses water  
C. Roasting is done in the microwave, while baking is done in the freezer  
D. Roasting and baking always mean exactly the same thing  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Roasting and baking both use oven heat, but roasting is often used for foods like vegetables or meats, while baking is often used for foods that set or rise.

### Mini Lesson 2: Roasting Needs Space

When roasting vegetables, space matters. If vegetables are piled on top of each other, they trap steam and may turn soft instead of browned.

Spreading vegetables in a single layer helps hot air reach more of the surface. This makes browning and crisp edges more likely.

#### Question 2

Why are vegetables often spread in a single layer before roasting?

A. To help them brown instead of steam  
B. To make them cook only on one side  
C. To keep them completely raw  
D. To make the oven colder  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A single layer gives vegetables more space and helps moisture escape. This makes browning easier.

### Mini Lesson 3: Baking Needs Accuracy

Baking is often less forgiving than other types of cooking. Ingredients like flour, sugar, eggs, baking powder, baking soda, and liquid work together to create the final texture.

If the measurements are too far off, baked goods may not rise, set, spread, or hold together correctly. Careful measuring helps the recipe turn out closer to the way it was designed.

#### Question 3

Why is accurate measuring especially important when baking?

A. Baking depends on ingredient balance for texture and structure  
B. Measuring makes food cook without heat  
C. Baking recipes do not use ingredients  
D. Measuring only changes the color of the oven  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Baking depends on the balance between ingredients. If that balance changes too much, the texture and structure can change.

# Category 4: Utensil Handling

## Skill: Essential Knife Work

### Mini Lesson 1: Protect Your Fingers

The safest way to hold food while slicing is to keep your fingertips tucked back. This is often called a claw grip.

With a claw grip, your fingertips curl under slightly while your knuckles face the knife. The side of the knife can rest lightly against your knuckles as a guide, while your fingertips stay out of the blade’s path.

#### Question 1

What is the safest way to hold food while slicing it with a chef's knife?

A. Curl your fingertips back in a claw grip  
B. Keep your fingers flat and stretched out  
C. Hold the food from the side closest to the blade  
D. Use your palm to press down on the blade  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A claw grip keeps your fingertips tucked back and helps protect them while slicing.

### Mini Lesson 2: Keep the Cutting Board Stable

A cutting board should not slide around while you are using a knife. If the board moves, the knife can slip and become harder to control.

To keep the board steady, place a damp towel or non-slip mat underneath it. This creates grip between the board and the counter.

#### Question 2

What should you place under a cutting board to keep it from sliding?

A. A damp towel or non-slip mat  
B. A pile of loose paper  
C. A sharp knife  
D. A dry onion peel  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A damp towel or non-slip mat helps hold the cutting board in place so it does not slide while cutting.

### Mini Lesson 3: Sharp Knives Are Safer Than Dull Knives

A sharp knife may sound more dangerous, but it is usually safer than a dull knife. A sharp knife cuts more easily, so you do not have to push as hard.

A dull knife can slip because it struggles to cut through food. When you press harder, it becomes easier to lose control. Keeping knives sharp helps make cutting smoother and safer.

#### Question 3

Why is a sharp knife usually safer than a dull knife?

A. It cuts more easily, so less force is needed  
B. It cannot cut anything  
C. It makes food cook faster  
D. It bends when it touches food  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A sharp knife cuts with less force. A dull knife can slip more easily because you have to push harder.

## Skill: Pan & Surface Matching

### Mini Lesson 1: Use Heavy Pans for Browning

Some foods need strong, steady heat to brown well. This is especially true when searing foods like steak, chicken, tofu, or vegetables.

A heavy pan, such as cast iron or stainless steel, is useful because it holds heat well. When the food touches the hot surface, it can develop a browned crust instead of just steaming.

#### Question 1

Which pan is best suited for searing a steak and developing a browned crust?

A. Cast iron skillet  
B. Plastic mixing bowl  
C. Thin disposable tray  
D. Small glass cup  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A cast iron skillet holds heat well, which helps steak develop a browned crust.

### Mini Lesson 2: Use Nonstick for Delicate Foods

Some foods are delicate and can tear or stick easily while cooking. Eggs are a common example because they cook quickly and can break apart when they stick to the pan.

A nonstick pan helps delicate foods release more easily. This makes it useful for scrambled eggs, omelets, pancakes, and other foods that need gentle handling.

#### Question 2

Which pan is generally best for cooking delicate eggs with minimal sticking?

A. Nonstick skillet  
B. Cast iron Dutch oven  
C. Baking sheet  
D. Metal colander  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A nonstick skillet helps delicate eggs release from the pan with less sticking.

### Mini Lesson 3: Use a Sheet Pan for Roasting

Roasting works best when food has enough space for hot air to move around it. A wide, shallow surface helps food brown instead of steam.

A baking sheet or sheet pan is a good choice for roasting vegetables because it spreads the food out in the oven. If the vegetables are piled too closely together, they may become soft instead of browned.

#### Question 3

Which baking surface is most appropriate for roasting vegetables until browned?

A. A sheet pan or baking sheet  
B. A deep cereal bowl  
C. A plastic container  
D. A coffee mug  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A sheet pan gives vegetables space so they can roast and brown instead of steaming in a crowded dish.

## Skill: Hack-it-With-What-You-Have

### Mini Lesson 1: Match the Shape and Job

You do not always need the exact kitchen tool a recipe mentions. Sometimes another clean, safe object can do the same job.

If you need to roll out dough, the tool needs to be smooth, sturdy, and easy to roll. A clean bottle or sturdy cup can work because it has a similar shape to a rolling pin.

#### Question 1

You need to roll out dough but do not own a rolling pin. What is the best substitute?

A. A clean bottle or sturdy cup  
B. A fork  
C. A paper towel  
D. A sponge  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A clean bottle or sturdy cup can roll over dough in a similar way to a rolling pin.

### Mini Lesson 2: Match the Action

When replacing a kitchen tool, think about the action the tool performs. A potato masher presses soft food down and breaks it apart.

If you do not have a potato masher, a sturdy fork can do a similar job. It may take longer, but it can still press and break up cooked potatoes.

#### Question 2

You need to mash potatoes but do not have a potato masher. What should you use?

A. A sturdy fork  
B. A drinking straw  
C. A plastic bag  
D. A measuring spoon only  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A sturdy fork can press and break apart soft potatoes, making it a useful substitute for a potato masher.

### Mini Lesson 3: Make Safe Setup Swaps

Some kitchen tools are really setups, not just single items. A double boiler is used to heat delicate foods gently, like chocolate, without placing them directly over strong heat.

A makeshift double boiler can be made by placing a heat-safe bowl over a pot of gently simmering water. The bowl should sit above the water, not directly in it. This lets steam warm the bowl gently.

#### Question 3

You need a makeshift double boiler to melt chocolate gently. Which setup is best?

A. A heat-safe bowl set over a pot of gently simmering water  
B. A plastic bowl directly on a burner  
C. Chocolate placed directly in a hot dry pan on high heat  
D. A paper plate over boiling water  

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: A heat-safe bowl over gently simmering water creates gentle heat, which helps melt chocolate without burning it.
